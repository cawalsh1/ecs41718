style="text-align:center;">Ruby Bridges

First African-American Child to Attend an all White School in the South

On November 14, 1960, Ruby Bridges' bravery led to the desegregation of all public schools in the South as she marched into an all white public school in the South, esccorted by U.S. Marshalls, with the intention to obtain an equal education as the other children around her.
At only six years old, she may as well be one of the youngest, yet just as powerful, activist for the civil rights movement.
Today, she shares her story with younger generations in educational forms.

  1. Ruby Bridges was born on September 8, 1954
  2. When she was four years old, she moved to New Orleans with her family
  3. In 1960, at age six, her family requested help from the NAACP for her role in the integration of schools
  4. At age seven, she became the first black student at an all white school
  5. Now, she works as a social activist and youth educator
